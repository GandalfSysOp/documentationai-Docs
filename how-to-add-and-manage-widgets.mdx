## **Widgets**

In ProofHub, you have the flexibility to customize your “**Me view**” to display the information most relevant to you. This is achieved through widgets, which allow you to control and organize the content you want to see at a glance.

## **Add widget**

1. **<u>Open widget options</u>**<u>:</u>\


   <Image src="https://help.proofhub.com/swift/wp-content/uploads/2024/05/Screenshot-2024-05-21-at-1.48.24-PM.png" width="1037" height="207" alt="" />

   - Click on the widget icon located right next to the “**Jump to**” option.

2. **<u>Drag and drop widgets</u>**<u>:</u>

   - In the “**Customize widgets**” window that appears, you’ll find a list of available widgets.\


     <Image src="https://help.proofhub.com/swift/wp-content/uploads/2024/05/Screenshot-2024-05-21-at-1.49.52-PM.png" width="1274" height="753" alt="" />

   - To add a widget, simply drag it from the list.\


     <Image src="https://help.proofhub.com/swift/wp-content/uploads/2024/05/Screenshot-2024-05-24-at-12.25.28-PM.png" width="1440" height="754" alt="" />

## **Resizing widget**

1. Click on the three dots in the top right corner of the widget you want to resize.\


   <Image src="https://help.proofhub.com/swift/wp-content/uploads/2024/05/Screenshot-2024-05-21-at-1.54.13-PM.png" width="1065" height="569" alt="" />

2. **<u>Select size option</u>**<u>:</u> From the menu that appears, choose either “**1x**” or “**2x**” to adjust the size of the widget.

   - **1x**: This option represents the default size of the widget.

   - **2x**: Selecting this option will increase the width and height of the widget, allowing for a larger display of information.

---

## **Remove widget**

Click on the three dots in the top right corner of the widget you want to remove and select “**Remove widget**“.\

## **My projects widget**

The “**My projects**” widget in ProofHub provides a quick overview of your assigned projects, displaying essential details such as the last update, start date, and end date. This guide will help you navigate and utilize the “**My projects**” widget effectively.

1. **<u>Tabs</u>**\


   <Image src="https://help.proofhub.com/swift/wp-content/uploads/2024/05/Screenshot-2024-05-21-at-3.50.50-PM.png" width="496" height="374" alt="" />

   - **Recent**:

     - Displays your most recently accessed projects, making it easy to quickly find and continue your work on current tasks.

   - **Starred**:

     - Allows you to mark important projects as “**Starred**” for easy access.

     - To star a project, click on the star icon next to the project name. Starred projects will then appear under the “**Starred**” tab for quick reference.

2. **<u>Filter options</u>**<u>:</u> You can filter your projects directly from the widget to find specific projects more easily. Click on the filter icon within the widget to open the filter options.\


   <Image src="https://help.proofhub.com/swift/wp-content/uploads/2024/05/Screenshot-2024-05-21-at-3.52.48-PM.png" width="513" height="374" alt="" />

   - **Status**: Filter projects by their status (e.g., Active, Completed).

   - **Category**: Filter projects by category to narrow down to specific types of projects.

   - **Color**: Filter projects by color coding, which is useful if you use color codes to differentiate project types or priorities.

## **Note widget**

Provides easy access to your personal notes, allowing you to jot down ideas, reminders, and important information.

1. **<u>Select notes to the widget:</u>**

   - In the widget, select the project, select the notebook, and the note.\


     <Image src="https://help.proofhub.com/swift/wp-content/uploads/2024/05/Screenshot-2024-05-21-at-4.20.14-PM.png" width="495" height="377" alt="" />

2. **<u>Select multiple notes</u>**<u>:</u>

   - You can select multiple notes to the widget by repeating the process of selecting the project, notebook, and note. This allows you to keep all relevant information easily accessible within the widget.\


     <Image src="https://help.proofhub.com/swift/wp-content/uploads/2024/05/Screenshot-2024-05-21-at-4.21.16-PM.png" width="1000" height="380" alt="" />
